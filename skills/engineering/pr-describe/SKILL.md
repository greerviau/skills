# pr-describe

Write a pull request body. This is the single source of truth for PR-body conventions — invoked standalone ("write the PR description for this branch") or as the body step of the `dev-workflow` skill.

It produces the body text only. Opening the PR, pushing, and watching CI belong to `dev-workflow`.

## Procedure

1. **Scope the branch.** Diff against the base to see what actually changed. The body describes the branch as it stands, not a running log of how it got there.
2. **Write an evergreen body** covering:
   - **Problem / request** — what the problem was or the feature being requested.
   - **Intent** — the goal of the change.
   - **Changes** — a concise summary of what was done.
   - **Testing** — how it was tested.
   - **Additional testing required** — anything a reviewer or QA should still exercise.
   - **Regressions** — known or potential regressions to watch for.

   If the repo has a PR body template, follow that, adding any additional information that's necessary or valuable.
3. **Enforce the exclusions.** The body is written once and stays evergreen. Do **not** include:
   - AI attribution of any kind — no "generated by" notes in the body, no agent co-author lines in commits.
   - References to specific version bumps or other narrow details that go stale and require constant updates.

## Boundaries

- Produces the body text only — does not open the PR, push, or watch CI.
- The PR *title* convention (`feat(...)`, `fix(...)`) and the "don't stop, watch CI" hand-off live in `dev-workflow`; they belong to the orchestration, not the body.
