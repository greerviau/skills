# open-pr

Open a pull request: write its title and body, then create it. This is the single source of truth for PR title and body conventions — invoked standalone ("open a PR for this branch") or as the PR step of the `dev-workflow` skill.

## Procedure

1. **Scope the branch.** Diff against the base to see what actually changed. The title and body describe the branch as it stands, not a running log of how it got there.
2. **Write the title.** Follow the `feat(...)` / `fix(...)` conventional-commit form, with a concise scope and summary (e.g. `fix(worktree): keep worktree alive until PR merges`).
3. **Write an evergreen body** covering:
   - **Problem / request** — what the problem was or the feature being requested.
   - **Intent** — the goal of the change.
   - **Changes** — a concise summary of what was done.
   - **Testing** — how it was tested.
   - **Additional testing required** — anything a reviewer or QA should still exercise.
   - **Regressions** — known or potential regressions to watch for.

   If the repo has a PR body template, follow that, adding any additional information that's necessary or valuable.
4. **Keep it evergreen.** The title and body are written once and stay accurate as the branch evolves. Do **not** include:
   - AI attribution of any kind — no "generated by" notes in the body, no agent co-author lines in commits.
   - References to specific version bumps or other narrow details that go stale and require constant updates.
5. **Open the PR** with the title and body (e.g. `gh pr create`).
